Import_role VS Include_role:
===== ========== ==========

1. import_role

Use import_role when you want the role tasks loaded at playbook parse time.

example:

---
- hosts: all

  tasks:
    - import_role:
        name: apache

Ansible loads role immediately.


2.include_role with condition:
---
- hosts: all

  tasks:
    - include_role:
        name: apache
      when: ansible_os_family == "RedHat"
	  
Here role runs only if system is RedHat.


include_role --> it will include tasks in run time, it will validate also in run time, it will not pre process.

Tags and when conditions apply only to the include_role statement itself, not the tasks within the role.

import_role --> ansible validate import_role before playbook execution
Tags and when conditions apply to the imported role and its tasks.

import_role loads role statically at parse time before execution.
include_role loads role dynamically at runtime during playbook execution.
include_role supports loops and conditions better.
